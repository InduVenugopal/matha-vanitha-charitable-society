Twenty-Five Years of Feeding the Forgotten

— M. Radhakrishna Pillai & Matha Vanitha Charitable Society

Every single day since March 1, 2000 — through hartals, public holidays, rain, and a pandemic — home-cooked meals have reached the indigent patients of Ward No. 9, Thiruvananthapuram General Hospital. Not once has a meal been missed.

The Story Behind the Mission

From a Pension &
a Pure Heart

M. Radhakrishna Pillai
Founder & Patron · Age 90

In 2000, M. Radhakrishna Pillai — a retired Superintendent from the Technical Education Directorate — received money from a land transaction. He and his wife, the late N. T. Indiramma, made a decision that would define the next quarter century of their lives: they would use their savings, and a good portion of Pillai's pension of ₹25,000, to feed the sick and forgotten.

"After retirement, I wanted to do something with the money I had got. It was then that somebody suggested Ward 9."

Ward No. 9 of Thiruvananthapuram General Hospital is where indigent patients — those with no family, abandoned on streets, brought in from other states — are admitted. When Pillai first walked in, the ward was in a distressing state. Patients had no clothes. Many had no plates to eat from. There were only two home nurses for hundreds of patients.

Pillai bought plates and glasses, brought home-cooked meals, and simply began. Matha Vanitha Charitable Society was formally established on March 1, 2000, under his patronage, initially driven by a group of committed women. His daughter R. Jayalekshmi became the Society's Secretary. His wife Indiramma, who passed away in 2005, was there at the very start.

The food is prepared every morning at Pillai's home — Lekshmi Mandiram, Kabarady Road, near Pettah. Cooks Radhamma and Usha start work at 7 AM. A regular meal is rice, two curries, and a pickle. By 12.45 PM, their devoted 'sarathy' Gopalakrishnan — who holds back all other auto trips at that hour — has delivered the food to Ward 9.

On hartal days, the hospital dispatches an ambulance to collect the meals. Not one single day's meal has ever been missed in over 25 years.

Beyond daily meals, the Society provides clothes, medicines, walking aids, wheelchairs, and television sets. Pillai also serves evening meals at Asha Bhavan, a home for destitute children in Poojappura, and distributes 10 kg of rice with medicines to visually challenged and cancer patients every weekend. He also gives financial assistance to students from underprivileged families.

Every Thiruvonam, the Society organises a grand Onasadya — all traditional dishes with two payasams — for every patient in the ward. It is the true spirit of Onam, lived out on a hospital floor every year.

"We haven't missed even a single day's lunch for the patients. Above all, there have been no complaints so far." — George Joseph, Member

Today, the ward has nine home nurses — up from just two when Pillai began. His persistent advocacy and the media coverage he helped generate transformed the ward. During COVID-19, even as the city locked down, Pillai continued to supply meals; the hospital superintendent eventually arranged daily ambulance pickup. Around 80 patients are now served daily. Pillai, now 90, has been felicitated by many organisations. His sons, daughter, and a circle of devoted friends continue the mission — his life's greatest work.

What the Society Provides

  • Home-cooked noon meals to ~80 patients of Ward 9 daily
  • Grand Onasadya every Thiruvonam
  • Evening meals to Asha Bhavan, Poojappura
  • 10 kg rice + medicines to visually challenged & cancer patients weekly
  • Clothes, walking aids, wheelchairs, TV sets for Ward 9
  • Financial aid to students from backward families
  • Onam kits (24 items) to home nurses of the ward

Supporters & Sponsors

  • State Bank of Travancore — sponsors meals on the 1st of every month
  • Well-wishers who sponsor sadyas on birthdays, anniversaries & memorials
  • Donors contributing from ₹100 to ₹50,000; all funds audited
  • Community donations of vegetables and provisions
What We Do

Our Mission in Action

Every initiative flows from one conviction: that no one — however poor, abandoned, or ill — should go hungry or uncared for.

🍱

Daily Noon Meals

Home-cooked rice, two curries and pickle prepared fresh every morning and delivered by autorickshaw to Ward No. 9 at 12.45 PM — every single day, without exception, since 2000.

🌸

Thiruvonam Onasadya

A sumptuous traditional feast with all customary dishes and two payasams, served to every patient in Ward 9 on Thiruvonam — Onam's spirit of giving, lived out every year.

👗

Clothes & Essentials

Clothing, plates, glasses, walking aids, wheelchairs, and television sets donated to patients who arrive with nothing at all — restoring dignity alongside nourishment.

🏠

Asha Bhavan Meals

Evening meals provided to Asha Bhavan, a home for destitute children in Poojappura — ensuring the most vulnerable children go to bed with a full stomach.

💊

Medicine & Rice

Every weekend, 10 kg of rice and essential medicines are given to visually challenged and cancer patients in the community — quiet, consistent, and unconditional.

🎓

Student Support

Financial assistance to students from backward and underprivileged families — carrying forward the belief that poverty's cycle must be broken through education.
